M140i
The M140i is part of the 1 Series class. It has a M-Performance Twin turbo 6-cylinder inline petrol engine which pushes out around 250kW of power and 500Nm torque. It is a 5-door car and is fitted with a 8-speed steptronic gearbox.
2 Series
With it's 3.0-litre displacement, the M Performance Twin Power Turbo inline 6-cylinder petrol engine of the BMW M240i is the top athlete in the convertible segment. Pushing out 250 kW of power and 500Nm of torque.
M2 CS
The M2 Competition Sport, being part of the M-family is the latest addition. This small coupe is fitted with a 6-cylinder inline Bi-turbo petrol engine, pushing out 302kW of power at 550Nm of torque. Fitted with M-performance technology it performs well.
M3 CS
M3 Sedan F80 Competition Sport, the most commmon and the most powerful of the M3-series. It has a 3.0 liter M Twin power turbo inline 6-cylinder petrol engine. It produces 338kW of power, fitted with M-steptronic dual clutch transmission.
4 Series
Powered by BMW TwinPower Turbo, TwinPower Turbo 4-cylinder or TwinPower Turbo 4-cylinder, it performs well. The customer are able to choose which of the three engines they'd want when they order their car. The optional 8-speed sport automatic transmission ensures maximum levels of dynamics, efficiency and ride comfort. Short shifting times improve the acceleration values while the fine spacing of the eight gears permits barely noticeable gear changing.
M4 CS
The M4 Competition Sport, one of the latest add ons to the M-Family is a sporty beast. Same as the F80 M3, it is powered by a 3.0 litre M Twin power turbo inline 6-cylinder petrol engine. The engine produces 317kW of power with 550Nm of torque. The twin-turbo direct injection and M tuning makes the slightest touch on the accelerator give a vigorous thrust, with excellent traction.
M5 CS
With the increased upgrade of power the M5 Competition pushes out 441kW of power along with 750Nm of torque. This is a major upgrade and the quickest of the set, being that it has so much torque. It is all powered by a M TwinPower Turbo 8-cylinder petrol engine. Installed is a 8-speed M Steptronic gearbox along with xDrive.
6 Series GT
Taking the place of the M6, the new updated 6 Series Gran Turismo, is the first of its kind. With a 4.0 litre turbocharged inline petrol engine with xDrive, it produces 250kW of power accompanied by 450Nm of torque. The design and aesthetic of the car is to drive long distances in style and comfort.
M760Li xDrive
Featuring a 12-cylinder 6.6 litre engine with M Performance TwinPower Turbo technology, the BMW M760Li xDrive is known as the ultimate 7 Series. Powered by the V12 engine it produces 448kW of power with a maximum of 800Nm of torque at 5000rpm's.
M8
The latest M on the block, the M8 powered by a twin-turbo 4.4-litre V8 developing 460kW of power and 750Nm of torque. An eight-speed M Steptronic transmission, which will produce power to all four wheels using Munich-based firm’s rear-biased M xDrive. It is believed to be the most powerful engine made by BMW yet.
i8 Coupé
The first hybrid produced by BMW performs to its ability. The coupé produces 275kW of power and 570Nm of torque. It speeds up from 0-100 km/h within 4.4s. It is the ultimate stylish futuristic car.
i8 Roadster
The BMW i8 Roadster reveals itself as futuristic right down to the last detail. Its progressive lines speak a formal language that can hardly be more dynamic. The most striking feature: the imposing lightweight carbon-fibre hinged doors, which can be opened comfortably. The athletic front continues to display the individual character of the car and proudly bears the BMW double kidney, flanked by full-LED headlights and redesigned Air Curtains. Pushing out 275kW of power along with 570Nm of torque at 6000 rpm's.
X3
The M Performance TwinPower Turbo inline 6-cylinder petrol engine of the BMW X3 M40i catapults the vehicle from zero to one hundred in an impressive 4.8 seconds. A maximum torque of 500 Nm and 265 kW of power it performs well being a 4.0 litre engine.
X5 M
This model car combines the M-Sport with a X-model car. Powered by a M TwinPower Turbo 8-cylinder petrol engine, with a 8-speed M Steptronic and M-specific xDrive, you'll get the sheer driving experience that BMW offers. Producing around 423kW of power with 750Nm of torque it goes from 0-100km/h in 4.2s.
X6 M
The BMW X6 M boasts two high-rev TwinScroll turbochargers. With its massive torque of up to 750 Nm, the 4.4 litre, 423 kW M TwinPower Turbo 8-cylinder petrol engine powers the BMW X6 M forward with minimum effort. It accelerates the vehicle from 0 to 100 km/h in just 4.2 seconds.
